Strategic Intelligence & Analysis

Decoding complex information to inform decisive action.

X Veritas Specialized Mastery

Course Overview

This course trains students to collect, analyze, and synthesize intelligence from multiple sources to support strategic decision-making in high-pressure environments. Emphasis is placed on critical thinking, source evaluation, and the use of advanced analytical frameworks.

Cadets will develop the ability to identify patterns, anticipate adversarial moves, and provide actionable insights to leadership.

Learning Objectives

  • Master intelligence cycle processes: collection, analysis, dissemination
  • Evaluate and validate intelligence sources
  • Apply analytical frameworks including SWOT, PESTLE, and link analysis
  • Use data visualization and modeling tools to uncover insights
  • Prepare strategic intelligence reports for diverse audiences

Mini Glossary

Term Definition
Intelligence Cycle The process of planning, collecting, analyzing, and disseminating intelligence.
SWOT Analysis Framework to identify Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
PESTLE Analysis Tool analyzing Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental factors.
Link Analysis Technique to map relationships between entities in intelligence data.
Counterintelligence Actions taken to prevent or thwart adversarial intelligence activities.
Scenario Building Developing possible future scenarios to guide strategic planning.
Source Validation Assessing the reliability and credibility of intelligence sources.
